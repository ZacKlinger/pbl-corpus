**Why this source matters:**
- The originating naming of "project" as a pedagogical unit in the U.S. context.
- Often cited as the intellectual ancestor of PBL (e.g., Condliffe 2017, p. 2: "Inspired by the philosophies of John Dewey, William Heard Kilpatrick developed the 'project method,' which is cited as the first formalization of a PBL model").
- One of the most-cited and most-misread texts in the lineage. Condliffe (p. 2) notes Kilpatrick's ideas "have been significantly revised since." The corpus's job here is to record what the 1918 text actually says — particularly Kilpatrick's argument about the "purposeful act" as the central unit — distinct from how it has been summarized in later PBL literature.
