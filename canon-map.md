---

## Empirical layer

Peer-reviewed cognitive science, ed psych, meta-analyses, RCTs, large-N implementation studies. Records **what has been measured** about PBL outcomes and what moderators have been identified.

### Meta-analyses and syntheses
- **Chen & Yang (2019)** — meta-analysis of project-based learning effects on academic achievement.
- **Condliffe et al. (2017, MDRC)** — *Project-Based Learning: A Literature Review.* The most-cited recent synthesis; surveys outcome studies and the implementation-quality moderator literature.

### RCTs and large implementation studies
- **Lucas Education Research / Knowledge in Action RCTs (Saavedra et al., 2021)** — AP Government / Environmental Science PBL studies.
- **Lucas Education Research / Multiple Literacies in PBL (Krajcik et al., 2023)** — third-grade science RCT.
- **Deeper Learning network outcomes studies (AIR, 2014; Zeiser et al.)** — quasi-experimental work on network-school outcomes.

### Implementation-grounded empirical
- **Mehta & Fine (2019)** — *In Search of Deeper Learning.* Practitioner-grounded empirical work documenting where deeper learning happens (and doesn't) inside U.S. high schools that aim for it.

### Hattie
- **Hattie (2009, 2017 updates)** — *Visible Learning*. The PBL-adjacent entries (problem-based learning, inquiry-based teaching) aggregate prior meta-analyses; for primary work, pull the underlying syntheses rather than the table alone.

## Visionary / philosophical layer

Records the **aims of PBL** and the theory of learning under it.

### Foundational
- **Dewey (1897)** — *My Pedagogic Creed.* Already in `learning-thesis/sources/`. Cross-reference rather than duplicate.
- **Dewey (1938)** — *Experience and Education.* The mature visionary articulation; the experience-as-curriculum lineage PBL descends from.
- **Kilpatrick (1918)** — *The Project Method.* The originating naming of "project" as a pedagogical unit. Often cited, often misread; the corpus's job is to record what the text actually says.

### Theory of learning under PBL
- **Bransford, Brown & Cocking, eds. (2000)** — *How People Learn* (NRC). The canonical synthesis that PBL claims about transfer, prior knowledge, and metacognition cite.
- **Vygotsky** — *Mind in Society* (ZPD, scaffolding). The theory of guided inquiry that practitioner frameworks invoke when they describe scaffolding.
- **Wiggins & McTighe (1998)** — *Understanding by Design.* The backward-design architecture under most PBL planning frameworks.

### Adjacent visionary
- **Bruner** — *The Process of Education* (1960), *The Culture of Education* (1996). Spiral curriculum and narrative meaning-making.
- **Freire** — *Pedagogy of the Oppressed.* The authenticity-of-task and problem-posing strands in PBL pull from Freire, often loosely.

## Practitioner layer

Records **how PBL is designed, taught, and assessed** in practice — the design intent of the field's leading frameworks and the documented practice of leading networks.

### PBLWorks family (Buck Institute lineage)
- **PBLWorks — Gold Standard PBL framework** (Larmer, Mergendoller & Boss, 2015 + ongoing updates). The seven essential elements.
- **HQPBL Framework** (High Quality Project Based Learning, 2018 coalition document). Six criteria for high-quality PBL.
- **PBLWorks rubrics** — Critical Thinking, Collaboration, Creativity & Innovation, Presentation, Self-management.
- **Larmer & Mergendoller — Essential Elements articles** and design rubrics.

### Adjacent execution frameworks
- **EL Education** — Berger et al., *Leaders of Their Own Learning*; *Learning That Lasts*; *Models of Excellence* archive. Core practices and the most-documented public record of student work at practitioner-defined quality.
- **High Tech High** — Daisy Martin, Rob Riordan; GSE Center for Research on Equity and Innovation case studies. Multi-year single-school documentation.
- **New Tech Network** — *NTN Design Principles*, *NTN learning outcomes rubrics*.

### Implementation accounts
- **Sizer (1984, 1992)** — *Horace's Compromise / Horace's School.* The original honest implementation account of secondary-school redesign toward deeper learning. (Already a `learning-thesis` candidate; cross-link.)
- **Boaler (2002, 2008)** — *Experiencing School Mathematics / What's Math Got to Do With It.* The Railside studies; equity-focused implementation record in problem-based math.

## Out of scope (for v1)

- **External critiques of PBL** (Kirschner/Sweller/Clark on minimal guidance; Hmelo-Silver/Duncan/Chinn rejoinder; Hirsch-style content-first arguments; Lemov direct-instruction practitioner case). These are adjudicative claims about whether PBL "works" — they belong in `learning-thesis/`, where AI/tech in education is being judged against the historical pattern. KSC is already in `learning-thesis/sources/`. Cross-link if a PBL claim here needs to acknowledge the external debate's existence; do not duplicate.
- **International PBL traditions** (Aalborg PBL, Maastricht PBL, McMaster-medical PBL, Olin engineering PBL) — distinct enough that they probably warrant their own corpus or a clearly scoped late thread.
- **Pre-Dewey antecedents** (Pestalozzi, Froebel, Parker) — historically relevant but not load-bearing for the v1 product context.
- **Maker education / FabLearn** — adjacent lineage; may enter later if the practitioner layer needs the case-study breadth.
