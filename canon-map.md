Three layers, each with different evidentiary weight (see `CLAUDE.md`). A claim's layer is the layer of the strongest source backing it.

---

## Empirical layer

Peer-reviewed cognitive science, ed psych, meta-analyses, RCTs, large-N implementation studies. Highest weight for causal claims about PBL outcomes.

### Meta-analyses and syntheses
- **Chen & Yang (2019)** — meta-analysis of project-based learning effects on academic achievement. Often cited as the headline effect-size source for PBL.
- **Condliffe et al. (2017, MDRC)** — *Project-Based Learning: A Literature Review.* The most cited recent synthesis; documents implementation variation as the dominant moderator.
- **Hattie (2009, 2017 updates)** — *Visible Learning*. PBL-adjacent entries (problem-based learning, inquiry-based teaching). The contested context: Hattie's effect sizes for inquiry/PBL are *not* glowing.

### RCTs and large implementation studies
- **Lucas Education Research / Knowledge in Action RCTs (Saavedra et al., 2021)** — AP Government/Environmental Science PBL studies.
- **Lucas Education Research / Multiple Literacies in PBL (MLPBL) (Krajcik et al., 2023)** — third-grade science RCT.
- **Deeper Learning network outcomes studies (AIR, 2014; Zeiser et al.)** — quasi-experimental but the load-bearing evidence for network-school deeper-learning claims.

### Contested but load-bearing
- **Kirschner, Sweller & Clark (2006)** — "Why minimal guidance during instruction does not work." Already in `learning-thesis`; the steelman against inquiry/PBL.
- **Hmelo-Silver, Duncan & Chinn (2007)** — the conventional rejoinder to KSC; whether it carries for K–12 PBL specifically is open.
- **Mehta & Fine (2019)** — *In Search of Deeper Learning.* Practitioner-grounded empirical work; the most honest reckoning with how rare deeper learning actually is, even in schools that explicitly aim for it.

## Visionary / philosophical layer

Highest weight for the aims of PBL and the theory of mind behind it.

### Foundational
- **Dewey (1897)** — *My Pedagogic Creed.* Already in `learning-thesis`. Cross-reference rather than duplicate; for this corpus, the relevant cut is Dewey on continuity of experience and the school as a form of community life.
- **Dewey (1938)** — *Experience and Education.* The mature articulation; the experience-as-curriculum thread that PBL descends from.
- **Kilpatrick (1918)** — *The Project Method.* The literal naming of "project" as a pedagogical unit. The originating document.

### Theory of mind and learning
- **Bransford, Brown & Cocking, eds. (2000)** — *How People Learn.* The canonical NRC synthesis that PBL claims about transfer, prior knowledge, and metacognition cite.
- **Vygotsky** — *Mind in Society* (ZPD, scaffolding). Often cited to defend the scaffolded inquiry that PBLWorks/EL describe.
- **Wiggins & McTighe (1998)** — *Understanding by Design.* Backward design as the architecture under most PBL planning frameworks. The product's interview workflow descends from this lineage.

### Adjacent visionary
- **Bruner** — *The Process of Education* (1960), *The Culture of Education* (1996). Spiral curriculum and the narrative construction of meaning.
- **Freire** — *Pedagogy of the Oppressed.* Authenticity-of-task threads in PBL pull from Freire's problem-posing pedagogy, often loosely.

## Practitioner layer

Highest weight for what actually works in real classrooms over time.

### PBLWorks family (Buck Institute lineage)
- **PBLWorks — Gold Standard PBL framework (Larmer, Mergendoller & Boss, 2015 + updates)**.
- **HQPBL Framework (High Quality Project Based Learning, 2018 coalition document)**.
- **Larmer & Mergendoller — Essential Elements** articles and design rubrics.

### Adjacent execution frameworks
- **EL Education core practices** — Berger et al., *Leaders of Their Own Learning*; *Learning That Lasts*. The EL family is arguably the strongest practitioner case for project-based work *with quality criteria*.
- **High Tech High** — Daisy Martin, Rob Riordan, the GSE Center for Research on Equity and Innovation case studies. Single-school but deeply documented.
- **New Tech Network** — *NTN Design Principles*, *NTN learning outcomes rubrics*.
- **Expeditionary Learning / Acumen Academy / Summit-style models** — where PBL meets school-redesign.

### Implementation accounts
- **Sizer (1984, 1992)** — *Horace's Compromise / Horace's School.* Already a `learning-thesis` candidate; the original honest implementation account.
- **Lemov (2010, 2021)** — *Teach Like a Champion.* The strongest practitioner counter to PBL-as-default. Direct-instruction execution at high fidelity.
- **Boaler (2002, 2008)** — *Experiencing School Mathematics / What's Math Got to Do With It.* The Railside studies; equity-focused implementation evidence for problem-based math.

## Out of scope (for now)

- International PBL traditions (Aalborg, Maastricht, McMaster-medical) — distinct enough that they probably warrant their own corpus or a clearly scoped thread.
- Pre-Dewey antecedents (Pestalozzi, Froebel, Parker) — historically relevant but not load-bearing for the current product context.
- Maker education / FabLearn — adjacent but a different lineage; might enter later if the practitioner layer needs the case study breadth.
