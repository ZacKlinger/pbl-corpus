# Claim-evidence: design elements of a PBL project

This thread catalogs what the practitioner frameworks and empirical syntheses describe as the design elements that distinguish a project from coursework. Practitioner-layer entries describe design intent; empirical-layer entries describe what implementation-and-outcomes research has measured about those elements.

---

## Claim: There is no field-wide consensus on what design elements constitute PBL; multiple practitioner frameworks coexist with overlapping but non-identical lists.

### Supporting evidence

- "a review of this literature reveals a continuing lack of consensus in the field on what must be present in a classroom for it to be considered a PBL classroom." — [Condliffe 2017](../sources/Condliffe-2017-pbl-literature-review.md) — p. 4 — layer: empirical
- "At the present time, however, there is a lack of agreement about what goes into high quality Project Based Learning." — [HQPBL 2018](../sources/HQPBL-2018-framework.md) — p. 1 — layer: practitioner
- "Since the model of PBL often drastically differs from study to study, it is very difficult to make research-based generalizations about PBL's effectiveness" — [Condliffe 2017](../sources/Condliffe-2017-pbl-literature-review.md) — p. 12 — layer: empirical

### Counter-evidence

- "some might argue that PBL can thrive as a dynamic concept without a concrete definition" — [Condliffe 2017](../sources/Condliffe-2017-pbl-literature-review.md) — p. 12 — layer: empirical (presented in Condliffe as a recognized counter-position within the field, not endorsed)

### Open questions

- Where do the seven PBLWorks Gold Standard elements and the six HQPBL criteria converge and diverge? Requires reading Gold Standard (Larmer/Mergendoller 2015) in primary text — currently a lead.
- How do the EL Education core practices and New Tech / High Tech High design principles overlay on these two BIE-lineage frameworks? Requires reading those frameworks in primary text.

## Claim: The HQPBL framework names six criteria written from the student-experience side: Intellectual Challenge and Accomplishment, Authenticity, Public Product, Collaboration, Project Management, and Reflection.

### Supporting evidence

- "Intellectual Challenge and Accomplishment: Students learn deeply, think critically, and strive for excellence." — [HQPBL 2018](../sources/HQPBL-2018-framework.md) — p. 2 — layer: practitioner
- "Authenticity: Students work on projects that are meaningful and relevant to their culture, their lives, and their future." — [HQPBL 2018](../sources/HQPBL-2018-framework.md) — p. 2 — layer: practitioner
- "Public Product: Students' work is publicly displayed, discussed, and critiqued." — [HQPBL 2018](../sources/HQPBL-2018-framework.md) — p. 3 — layer: practitioner
- "Collaboration: Students collaborate with other students in person or online and/or receive guidance from adult mentors and experts." — [HQPBL 2018](../sources/HQPBL-2018-framework.md) — p. 3 — layer: practitioner
- "Project Management: Students use a project management process that enables them to proceed effectively from project initiation to completion." — [HQPBL 2018](../sources/HQPBL-2018-framework.md) — p. 4 — layer: practitioner
- "Reflection: Students reflect on their work and their learning throughout the project." — [HQPBL 2018](../sources/HQPBL-2018-framework.md) — p. 4 — layer: practitioner
- "The Framework for High Quality Project Based Learning describes PBL in terms of the student experience and is intended to provide educators everywhere with a shared basis for designing and implementing good projects." — [HQPBL 2018](../sources/HQPBL-2018-framework.md) — p. 1 — layer: practitioner

### Open questions

- How do the HQPBL criteria correspond to the PBLWorks Gold Standard's design from the teacher side (challenging problem/question, sustained inquiry, authenticity, student voice and choice, reflection, critique and revision, public product)? Some criteria appear in both lists with different names and granularity; others (Project Management) appear prominently in HQPBL but not as a Gold Standard element.

## Claim: The project is the central vehicle of instruction in PBL, not a culminating activity attached to traditional coursework.

### Supporting evidence

- "What clearly distinguishes PBL from other instructional approaches is that projects are not the culmination of learning (as they often are in standard classrooms), but instead are the process through which learning takes place." — [Condliffe 2017](../sources/Condliffe-2017-pbl-literature-review.md) — p. 6 — layer: empirical
- "projects must be the 'spine of the course' and should be thought of as the 'main course, not dessert'" — [Condliffe 2017](../sources/Condliffe-2017-pbl-literature-review.md) — p. 6–7, quoting Parker et al. (2013) — layer: empirical

### Open questions

- The Condliffe review notes that no design framework specifies how long a PBL unit must last to count as PBL, or what share of a course PBL must occupy. This boundary is unresolved in the field.

## Claim: "Need to know" is contested as a design element — Buck Institute removed it from the Gold Standard list in 2015, arguing it is an outcome of design rather than a design move.

### Supporting evidence

- "Cultivating a 'need to know' was a design principle for the BIE until 2015 when the Institute intentionally removed 'need to know' from its design principles list. While the BIE acknowledged that this component is 'one of the most powerful arguments for PBL,' it also believes that 'this term belongs in a "Why PBL?" argument, not as a thing teachers design in a project the way they would, say, an authentic product or opportunities for student voice and choice'" — [Condliffe 2017](../sources/Condliffe-2017-pbl-literature-review.md) — p. 8, footnote 12, citing Larmer and Mergendoller (2015b, p. 3) — layer: empirical (reporting on practitioner-layer revision)

### Open questions

- New Tech Network still uses "Need-to-Know" (NTK) as a central instructional move (per Condliffe 2017, p. 19). This is an internal practitioner-layer disagreement between BIE/Gold Standard and NTN about whether the construct is a design element or an outcome.
